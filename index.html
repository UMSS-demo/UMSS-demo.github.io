Enhancing Unified Modality Sound Separation

with Multi-Modality Joint Queries

Abstract

Sound separation is a crucial component of signal processing and finds applications in diverse fields like speech recognition, speech enhancement, and music analysis. Traditional methods typically involve training dedicated models for individual modal queries, restricting their ability to handle multi-modal composed queries effectively. To overcome this constraint, we introduce a novel approach called Omni-modal Sound Separation \textbf{(OmniSEP)}, capable of performing sound separation across queries of various modalities. We introduce a \texttt{Query-Mixup} strategy, blending various modal queries during training to create a unified representation, facilitating sound separation across modalities. Moreover, we explore utilizing noise information by introducing negative queries, enabling more flexible sound separation. Additionally, our \texttt{Query-Aug} strategy employs retrieval augmentation to address limitations with restricted text queries during training, improving robustness to out-of-domain queries. Experimental evaluations on the MUSIC, VGGSOUND-CLEAN+, and MUSIC-CLEAN+ datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of OmniSEP, achieving state-of-the-art performance across Text-, Image-, and Audio-Queried Sound Separation tasks. The results obtained by employing negative queries across the three tasks also demonstrate the capability of our omniSEP to perform sound separation based on multi-modal combined queries.
